VOICE OF A VULNERABLE CHILD KENYA

The project by a researcher at Kenya, found that institutional practices and Traditional practices can lead to a highly gendered home, church and school environment rarely challenged by children. By condoning sexual abuse and aggressive behavior, community communicates to children that violence is a normal feature of life. Schools homes and churches can provide an enabling environment for the prevention of gender-based violence The main objective of the proposed project is to provide quality counselling services and support to children and youth who are victims of school, family or church related gender based violence. The grant requested will also be used to provide training in counselling to stakeholders of the targeted communities in order to enable them to counsel the children themselves. The target population is 5 counties selected in the country; Makueni, Machakos, Kitui, Nairobi and Kilifi. One of the counties (Makueni) will be used as a control group and the remaining four (4) counties (Machakos, Kitui, Kilifi and Nairobi) will be used as the sample size.


Voice of a Vulnerable Child Kenya (VVC) was established in response to an increase of violence against women, children and teenage pregnancy during this time of COVID 19 pandemic. The organization will offer training and development programs for services providers, including legal and medical practitioners, Peer educators, counselors, law enforcement agencies and community based counselors. VVC Kenya in collaboration with other stake holders will start outreach programs on SGBV to schools, churches and in the community. Through the interactive weekly sessions, children will learn to recognize and appropriately respond to violence in their homes, schools churches and in the community at large.

VVC MAIN GOAL

Psychological counseling, supporting survivors and training are core mandates of V.V.C Kenya. Counselling is the process of helping people to realize the potentials in them to solve their problems. It is important for V.V.C Kenya to seek welfare of its clients and make children understand that they are heard, thought of, taken serious and most importantly are believed in. The main goal in this area is to create a confidential and safe platform for school girls and boys survivors to air their feelings and experiences with a counselor who is equipped with the knowledge and skills in helping children to cope with their situation, through group counselling and individual counseling. Through the participation in supportive counseling and VVC clubs in schools and churches, survivors will realize they are not alone in their struggle against home, school and church violence and they are able to learn new strategies for taking control over their lives.

Project Objective

Our broad objectives are:

  1. To increase opportunities that will create a forum for sharing ideas, experience and learning about SRGBV among community members (children, parents, teachers, pastors and other stakeholders)
  2. To provide services and referrals system for victims and their parents or guardians
  3. To provide full support to teenagers who end up getting pregnancy due to SGBV
  4. • To provide speak out to men, especially in (casino areas, boda boda men, or men conference areas i.e church conference or during chief baraza
